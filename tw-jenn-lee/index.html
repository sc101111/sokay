TimeLine

  • Part I

    Jenn Lee was deeply affected after visiting the World War II, Nazi concentration camp during her stay in Germany. Upon her return, she started a series of collection. Together with contemporary
    dance groups, they used these garments in an attempt to search, communicate and find the voice of the youths in this generation—Freedom. Do we exist in a "Concentration Camp" of this
    era? As the concentration camps hosted the Jews in Germany during World War II, the collective of fashion artists reimagined the setup and scene with photographs and videos of these concentration camps.
    This gave rise to the concept that inspired ideas such as blood, torn bodies and the fettering of physical and mental states. The dancers envision themselves back to this space and period of time to create
    their own roles, reinventing body movements in their own interpretations. How did they do it? By manifesting their most primal instincts—fear. The portrayal of uncontrollable madness akin to a
    wild beast which they can express in their performances with no restrictions; their heads, hands, fingers, even toes and every inch of their own skins singing and roaring to their personas. Like an eight
    year old boy, brought up by a beast with the brutal experience of endless rapes, ultimately filling him with anxiety and anger towards humankind. Or the elegant lady, with her loved ones dying one by
    one in front of her eyes. With all these characters coming together and dressed in costumes that speak their roles, the dancers, their very own interpretations and the garments intertwined to express
    and discover the depths of this historical tragedy. Is only dancing elegantly on a stage can we call it a dance? On the same note, are garments worn by beautiful models on a runway do
    we call it garments?

  • Part II

    We searched and explored the possibilities between garments and dance in this performance. Garments are an art form that stands the test of time, and dances are sparkles of a moment. When in tandem, a
    dance gives life to garments, and garments exude new perspective to dancers. This, we aim to look for possibilities within limitations. “The reason why I’m attracted to art is because of its varieties and
    inclusivity. History and memory won’t just stay in the past. It follows time and gives memories to different people; read and translated by different thoughts. I don’t want to merely use dance to represent how
    concentration camps are, or to interpret it as how it was in that period of time. This is to let dancers feel and understand how and what concentrated camps mean solely to us. More importantly, the
    reasons behind our very own “concentrated camp,” the lead dancer said.

    The core of this creation stems from fashion design combined with contemporary dance. Creator Jenn Lee documented the process of working with different artists—one idea with differing visual
    interpretations. A collaboration that also joined hands with multi-media and visual artists, shoulder to shoulder creating still and moving visuals altogether. 

  • Part III

    JENN LEE was established in 2015, based in Taipei, Taiwan. Designer Lee graduated from London College of Fashion in 2011. Before started JENN LEE, Lee interned at Alexander McQueen under Gucci Group, and London based fashion designer Felicity Brown. She tries to find the perfection within imperfection, and to discover the missing pieces from all the broken and chaotic remains. Freedom, Love and Peace.
